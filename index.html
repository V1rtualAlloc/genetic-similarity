Interpreting the Scores

Cosine similarity measures the direction of genetic drift between two populations in 25-dimensional space. Unlike Euclidean distance, it is less sensitive to the absolute magnitude of the components and focuses on the "ancestral vector."

Score Meaning G25 Context
+1.0 Perfectly Similar Identical genetic direction; essentially the same population.
0.99+ Very Close Highly related groups (e.g., Germanic vs. Celtic). Consider merging labels.
0.0 Orthogonal No shared directional relationship; genetically independent vectors.
-1.0 Polar Opposites Inverse genetic profiles (e.g., pure Northern European vs. pure East Asian).

Note: In G25 data, scores below 0 are rare unless comparing vastly different continental poles (like Huns vs. Phoenicians).
